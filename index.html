Wicked Willow · Atchison Gulch

Explore each location, read the letters & spells, then solve the riddle. Each success mints a coin. Collect all five coins to perform the final ritual and lift Willow’s curse.

1 · The Ridge

Ashes & Echoes

The night the mine collapsed, Willow lit her broken lantern and spoke to the wind. Some say the hills still hold her words.

Lantern Note, found on the ridge

“Truth warned you, but greed made you deaf. If light returns to the lantern, the path might yet be seen.”

Riddle: I’m not alive, yet I grow. I need the breath of air, yet I fear the fall of rain. What am I?

Hint: It burns in lanterns and eats what it’s fed.

2 · Mine Mouth

The Canary’s Warning

Families came to Willow in secret. Miners asked which shafts were safe. She told them to watch the birds.

Miner’s Pocket Letter

“If the song stops, turn back. No vein of coal is worth a widow’s veil.”

Riddle: I sing to save and die to warn. In darkness, I am a sentinel. What am I?

Hint: A tiny guardian of underground air.

3 · The Dry River

Salt & Bones

The riverbed cracked after Willow’s curse. What once murmured now keeps its mouth full of dust.

River-Binding Spell (scratched in silt)

“Break my course and still my tongue; leave only salt upon my lungs.”

Riddle: I run but cannot walk. I have a bed but never sleep. I have a mouth, yet never eat. What am I?

Hint: You’re standing in it—though now it’s dry.

4 · The Cottage

The Mayor’s Head

Mayor Tobias Hargrove mocked Willow until the day he lost everything—except his voice.

Whisper from the Box

“Heh… look what she left me—only weight on my words.”

Riddle (the Mayor speaks): Forwards I’m heavy; backwards I’m not. What am I?

Hint: Spell me in reverse and I vanish in weight.

5 · Town Square

The Accusation

In the square, the crowd broke Willow’s lantern and spat her name into the dust. She left a final poem—read the first letters down.

Willow’s Poem (Acrostic)

We begged the hills for more than they would give.

I warned you softly, but you loved the earth too loud.

Looters of luck, you called it labor, called it life.

Lantern light is guidance, not a guarantee.

Only a fair pact will free this place again.

Will you honor what was broken?

Question: Read the acrostic (first letters). Enter the single word Willow asks for to end the feud.

Hint: Take the first letter of each line and read downward.

Ritual Locked — collect 5 coins.
Coins mint when you answer each section. Use “Hints” if you’re stuck.

Final Rite of Unbinding

“Lantern rekindled, river released; song restored, and judgment ceased.
From ridge to square, let grudges part—
Return what’s owed and mend the heart.”

Hold up your five coins, speak the rite together, and tap the button below to lift the curse.

About This Game

Designed as a QR-code friendly party game. Each section reveals lore and a riddle; correct answers mint coins. Collect all five to unlock the group ritual.
